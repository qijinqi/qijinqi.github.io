Publications and Current Works

  1. When School Ties Meet Geography: Education-Province Bias in Mutual Fund Portfolios (Liang, Quanxi, Qi Jin*, Meiting Lu, and Yaowen Shan)

    - Journal of Banking & Finance, 2023, Available online [DOI: 10.1016/j.jbankfin.2023.107021]

    • Mutual fund managers tend to overweight stocks headquartered in provinces where they received education, leading to poorer performance and higher idiosyncratic risks. The overweighting is due to familiarity bias instead of information.

  2. Navigating Climate Changes: How Do Adaptation Initiatives Affect Corporate Debt Choice? (Boubaker, Sabri, Qi Jin, Xiaoran Ni, Chi Zhang)

    - Working Paper, 2023, in preparation for a top-tier finance journal [Latest Version]

    • Employing the staggered introduction of state-level climate change adaptation plans as quasi-exogenous shocks for perceived climate risks, we find that affected firms rely more on private debt financing.

  3. Institutional Investors’ Limited Attention and Stock Price Informativeness in Emerging Markets: Evidence from China (Ni, Xiaoran*, Qi Jin)

    - Pacific-basin Finance Journal, 2023, Revise & Resubmit [Latest Version]

    • Because of limited attention, institutional investors distraction decreases the firm’s stock price informativeness

  4. Environmental Regulation and the Cost of Debt: Evidence from the Carbon Emission Trading System Pilot in China (Ni, Xiaoran, Qi Jin, and Kunhao Huang*)

    - Finance Research Letters, 2022 [DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2022.103134]

    • The staggered implementation of carbon emission trading system increases cost of debt
